Methodology

To quantify this dialogic discourse, an XML encoding of the text in the original Russian was produced, tagging all instances of speech, as well as their attributes (direct vs. indirect, timid vs. self-confident, et cetera); moreover, to formalize the encoding, RelaxNG was used to write a schema, ensuring that the encoding is not only well-formed, but also adheres to the specifications of our analysis. Regular expressions were used to clean the text and to locate grammatical indications of speech (angled quotation marks or dashes). Following the location of speech acts, each act's attributes were defined. We then counted how often each character (Goliadkin, Goliadkin's double, the narrator) engages in the three types of speech, as well as how Goliadkin's speech acts shift in emotional valence over the course of the work.
