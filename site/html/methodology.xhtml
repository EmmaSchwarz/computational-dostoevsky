Methodology

To quantify this dialogic discourse, an XML encoding of the text in the original Russian was produced, tagging all instances of speech, as well as their attributes (speaker, addressee, voice type); moreover, to formalize the encoding, RelaxNG was used to write a schema, ensuring that the encoding is not only well-formed, but also adheres to the specifications of our analysis. Following the location of speech acts, each act's attributes were defined. We then counted how often Goliadkin engages in the three speech voicings (timid, confident, and mocking), as well as the patterns of his internal and external discourse. The discourse of others was analyzed through focusing on the transference of words across characters. A combination of XSLT and SVG was used to generate the visualizations, and XSLT was further used to transform the XML encoding of the text into the full view of The Double found on the "Text" page.
